    02 Apr 2024, 00:00

    Between opaque tights, knee-high socks, and maxi-length everything, it seems modesty is having a moment. And while even the best thigh-high boots seem like the antithesis of shy girl fashion, the high-legged silhouette allows for smart layering and can be significantly more wearable than you’d think. Just let celebs like Rihanna and Amal Clooney — both of whom were spotted in the bold shoe lately — guide the way.

    “Thigh-high boots continue to be a top trend because they create a bold statement with their dramatic length,” says Michaela Leitz, a stylist based in Germany. “They’re fantastic for elongating the silhouette and adding a touch of elegance to any outfit.”

    Even if you’re not trying to elongate anything, over-the-knee boots offer a fun and unexpected way to add a little coverage to a mini skirt during the transition from summer to fall — or even a striking alternative to your go-to knee-high boots with jeans or a sweater dress. Brands like Burberry, Bally, and Stella McCartney all sent thigh-high footwear down the runway for Fall/Winter 2023 — and experts agree that they’re not going anywhere.

    Best Overall

    Naturalizer Lyric Over-the-Knee Boot

    Nordstrom

    What We Love: These come in regular and wide for every size.

    What We Don’t Love: The inside zip isn’t completely hidden.

    The slightly flared block heel on these Naturalizer boots might be the first thing you’ll notice, but look closer and you’ll notice the smooth, buttery leather, square toe, and asymmetrical opening that make this timeless over-the-knee style our favorite overall pick. Even more, once you put your foot inside, you’ll realize the built-in arch support with a molded heel cup and hidden goring — the elastic panel that creates a snug fit without the need for shoelaces — gives these books a sneaker-like feel.

    Size: 6–12 | Colors: Black | Material: Leather upper; synthetic lining and sole | Heel Height: 3.25 inches

    Best Budget

    ASOS Kayla Heeled Thigh High Boots

    ASOS

    What We Don’t Love: The stiletto heel doesn’t have much traction.

    Save these four-inch stilettos for a mild winter day — the sleek, elegant heel doesn’t have much traction for an entire day’s wear, but they sure look good for a dinner date or a drink close by. Made from smooth faux leather with a fabric lining, these over-the-knee boots have a touch of structure and an asymmetrical opening at the thigh.

    Size: 4–11 | Colors: Black | Material: Faux leather | Heel Height: 4 inches

    What to Keep in Mind

    Material

    Thigh-high boots are notoriously difficult to put on — especially when they hug the leg, like many of the boots featured in this story. More malleable materials, like suede and synthetics, might make the boots easier to slide on, while stiffer leathers could require a bit of tugging in order to get them on (and off).

    Zippers and Closures

    To make them easier to put on, look for an external zipper — usually hidden on the inside of the leg — that allows the shoe to have more give when slipping them on. Many boots, like this Stuart Weitzman choice (not featured on the above list but still one of the brand's core styles), also feature a tie around the top of the boot to keep them snug to the thigh without slipping down. But of course, those with an intended slouchy fit, like Schutz's Ashlee boot, have that appeal, so just be mindful of the style before purchasing.

    Your Questions, Answered

    How do you style over-the-knee boots?

    When it comes to pants, pair thigh-high boots with skinny jeans, so the boots can be seen — after all, what's the point of wearing them if you can't see them? But we've been seeing more influencers and celebrities — Megan Fox, Dua Lipa, and Khloe Kardashian, to name a few — wearing the boots with mini and midi skirts for a more layered look.

    What socks should you wear with over-the-knee boots?

    Stuart Weitzman’s Global Head of Design, Edmundo Castillo, suggests the least chunky sock possible when slipping into over-the-knee boots. “Whenever I’m working with over-the-knee boots, whether with developing prototypes on a fit model or connecting with customers in stores, I always suggest wearing a thin sock.” Not only is a thinner sock more comfortable, he points out, but “it can also preserve the inner lining of the boots to help make them last season after season.”

    How do you keep thigh-high boots up?

    While most quality thigh-high boots are made with substantial enough leather or suede to stay up on their own, there’s always a chance they could slip or fall. If the boot doesn’t have an adjustable tie-back, the best way to ensure they stay up is with specialized tape or adhesive. Double-sided fashion tape made for the skin is your best bet — but if you’re going out dancing or planning on sweating, try using wig tape, which is better designed to handle moisture and movement. (When worn over bare legs, we recommend shaving the spot beforehand to be sure no hair gets caught in the tape.) Be sure to apply the tape while standing in at least two spots, and when the night is over, use a bit of oil to remove the adhesive. If you’re tucking your pants into your boots, this tape from Hollywood Fashion Secrets works like a charm.
