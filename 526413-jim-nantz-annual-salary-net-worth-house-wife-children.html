    Jim Nantz’s biography

    The personality was born on 17 May 1959 in Charlotte, North Carolina, United States. His parents are Jim Nantz Jr and Doris Nantz. He has one sister, Nancy Hockaday.

    He grew up in different parts of the United States, such as New Orleans, Louisiana, Colts Neck Township, New Jersey, and Marlboro Township. He schooled at Marlboro High School, where he played basketball and golf.

    After high school graduation, he enrolled at the University of Houston. He graduated in 1981 with a Bachelor of Arts in Radio and Television Broadcasting.

    Career

    After graduating from the University of Houston, Jim started working as a sportscaster and anchor for KHOU in Houston, Texas. Between 1982 and 1985, he became a weekend sports reporter on KSL-TV in Salt Lake City, calling BYU football and Utah Jazz games.

    In 1985, Jim joined CBS Sports, initially as a studio host for the network’s college basketball and football coverages. He was an on-course reporter for PGA Tour Golf and called NFL games on Westwood One from 1988 to 1990 before moving to television, calling Sunday Night Football.

    Wife and children

    Jim married his first wife, Ann Lorraine Carlsen, in 1983. They lived in Westport, Connecticut and had a daughter. After staying together for 26 years, they divorced in 2009. He was ordered to pay $916,000 a year to his ex-wife in child support and alimony.

    Close to three years later, he married Courtney Richards. The couple exchanged their wedding vows on 9 June 2012 at the Pebble Beach Golf Links in Pebble Beach, California. Later, Courtney and Jim Nantz's daughter was born in 2014, while their son was born in 2016.
