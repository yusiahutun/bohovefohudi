    Top 20 female Nascar drivers in history

    Recently, a band of women has defied the female NASCAR racing stereotyping. By outshining them in other instances, they have stood as a testament that female drivers are just as competent as their male counterparts. Here is a list of the best female NASCAR drivers' names.

    20. Danica Patrick

    • Full name: Danica Sue Patrick
    • Date of birth: 25 March 1982
    • Age: 42 years old (as of 2024)
    • Place of birth: Beloit, Wisconsin, United States

    Danica Patrick has cemented her name as the best female NASCAR driver ever. She was also named in the Times 100 most influential people list. She achieved legendary status owing to her 7 top 10 finishes in the Xfinity Nascar Series, making her one of the most decorated female drivers.

    Her passion for sports made her drop out of school to focus on auto-racing as a full-time career. Danica Patrick's NASCAR win in the IndyCar Series 300 in Japan in 2008 cemented her legendary status, becoming the first female driver to win a championship.

    19. Shawna Robinson

    • Full name: Shawna Robinson
    • Date of birth: 30 November 1964
    • Age: 59 years old (as of 2024)
    • Place of birth: Des Moines, Iowa, United States

    Iowa U.S born Shawna Robinson is among the pioneering female drivers in NASCAR. Now retired, she debuted the competition in the Spring Cup Series in 1995 and followed up with a Camping World Truck debut in 2003. Robinson is carried around in high regard following her three times Dash Series wins between 1988 and 1989.

    The touring series fete placed her in the class of only four women alongside Hailie Deegan, Manami Kobayashi and Gracie Trotter. Besides attaining a top-ten finish in the NASCAR Xfinity Series, Robinson's accolades include completing the ARCA Bondo and Daytona Dash Series.
