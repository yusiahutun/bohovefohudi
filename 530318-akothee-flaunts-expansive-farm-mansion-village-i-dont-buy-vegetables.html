    • She surveyed the grounds, making observations and giving instructions to the property's managers on the changes she would like made

    Kenyan musician and businessperson Akothee has displayed her vast farm in Rongo, Migori county.

    Akothee's farm and mansion in Rongo

    In a series of videos on her Instagram, she walked around the farm, flaunting its magnitude and the several crops grown there, such as eggplants and sukumawiki.

    "Oh my! Guys look at my farm. Look at that. My goodness! Nelly must come and see this," she said, showing off the huge eggplants on the farm.

    "I live in the village, and I am a farmer. How much do you guys buy egg plant for?" she added teasing Nairobi peeps.

    Akothee's advice to single mothers

    The mother of five then advised single mothers to build homes for themselves and their children no matter how small to live comfortably.

    "For all the single mothers in the house, it's time we organize ourselves. I told you as a single mother in 2024 if you can't build a mansion, build something small for you and the babies. So you can have a small kitchen garden....this is called a kitchen garden..look at how my kitchen garden looks. I don't buy courgettes, I don't buy potatoes...I don't need to buy vegetables," she said.

    In another video, Akothee walked around her multimillion-shilling mansion, surveying it and giving instructions to the managers in charge of the property.

    "When I just leave my home behind for a week," she captioned the video referring to the state of the property.

    The mansion is estimated to cost KSh 80 million and contains modern amenities, including a pool, well-manicured lawns, and a jacuzzi.

    Akothee's net worth and properties

    Akothee is known for her wealth, which she has never been afraid of flaunting, and her strong business acumen.

    As of 2022, numerous media houses, including The 360 Report, indicated that the singer and businesswoman was worth about KSh 1.533 billion ($10 million).

    She has multiple sources of income, including her tours and travel business, music, and real estate.

    For personal use, besides the Rongo mansion, she owns a palatial home in Mombasa.

    The property stands on the beachfront and has an estimated worth of over 100 million Kenyan shillings.

    The property features a swimming pool, multiple bathrooms, many spacious bedrooms, a kitchen, and a lounge.
