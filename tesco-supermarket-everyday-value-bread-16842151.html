    Tesco SHAKE-UP: Why supermarket STOPPED selling its everyday value bread

    Brits living on a low budget would have gone to the bakery aisle only to find the 45p everyday value bread is no longer on the shelves. The supermarkets budget everyday value range includes cupboard staples like beans, bread and eggs.

    16 Feb 2024, 00:00

    Brits living on a low budget would have gone to the bakery aisle only to find the 45p everyday value bread is no longer on the shelves.

    The supermarket’s budget everyday value range includes cupboard staples like beans, bread and eggs.

    But after a huge shake-up, the food store has banished the brand from its shelves.

    The reason for it is because it has been replaced by loaves of H.W. Nevill’s for the same price.

    Packets of H.W. Nevill’s bread cost 45p for an 800g loaf, even though some shoppers spotted them selling for 40p in stores last week.

    Sadly for bread-lovers, the supermarket confirmed that it will no longer be selling the everyday value loaf – although the new bread will cost the same.

    It is still the cheapest loaf of white bread on the shelves – the next cheapest is the Tesco small toastie bread 400g for 50p.

    H.W. Nevill’s was London’s largest bakery back in 1872. But after hitting hard times in 1970, it was bought by Associated British Foods who also produce Kingsmill.

    A Tesco spokesperson said: “We’re always looking at how we can offer our customers great quality products at great value prices.

    “As part of this, a number of Tesco products, including sliced bread, will be getting a new look and feel to complement their fantastic quality.

    “We’ve also added to our range of brands with the introduction of H.W. Nevill’s bread.”

    This comes after the supermarket giant announced to cut 1,700 shop floor jobs across its UK stores.
