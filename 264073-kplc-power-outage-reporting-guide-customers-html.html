    There is nothing as disappointing as a power outage when your favorite game or soap opera is just about to kick off. For business owners, a blackout may mean lost money or closing shop early. Some of the common causes of power outages in Kenya include weather conditions, trees, vandalism, animals, accidents, planned interruptions, and faulty equipment. Strong winds may also affect your power supply as they lead to conductor fault resulting to lights going on and off within seconds. Trees that fall on the power line have a similar effect as the winds. Luckily, for these cases (trees and wind), the breaker protection may clear the fault in no time. In some cases, the KPLC equipment such as an overloaded transformer may disrupt power. At times, accidents may affect poles and cause a KPLC Kenya power outage.

    By now, you should have noticed that any time there are heavy rains, the likelihood of a blackout increases. Well, this is because rain reduces insulation properties of the power line materials (porcelain bushings) that are essential for keeping the flow of electricity. So, when water penetrates the line, it causes shorting leading to a blackout. Also, Kenya Power lets you plan by providing a list of scheduled areas to have outages beforehand. If you want to know about any planned KPLC power outage today, you can visit the website www.kplc.co.ke or Facebook and Twitter (Kenya Power Alerts) or buy any of the dailies to read about gazette areas. Alternatively, you can register with Kenya Power Alerts to receive updates on scheduled blackouts. Anytime your region is set to have an outage you will receive a notification via direct message or email.

    Reporting Power Outage to KPLC

    But, there is hope. Kenya Power and Lighting Company hopes to avert this problem by providing you a platform to communicate when an outage happens. Below is a guide on KPLC power outage reporting.

    SMS customer service

    Alert customer care about power disruption in your area through KPLC power outage SMS line 95551. KPLC requires that you indicate your meter number/account number, and, the nature of your problem. An SMS may look like Stima#98765345# no power. But if you are reporting an outage due to faulty lines and equipment you may need to provide a detailed description. For example, you could write Stima# 875634231# no power due to a defective power line on Ronald Ngala near Naivas Supermarket. Include any landmarks such as roads, schools, churches or building that may help the team in solving the problem. Once KPLC receives the SMS, they will send you another message showing the reference number of your complaints and the steps they will take. The SMS will cost you five shillings above the standard rate.

    Use Social Media

    Kenya Power has one of the most active social media accounts among all the other public corporations. On Twitter and Facebook, you can reach out to KenyaPower_care for any queries or complaints. When reporting a power outage on Facebook or Twitter, Kenya Power requires that you provide your account number and nature of the problems. Immediately, you will receive feedback on your complaint and the way forward. Nevertheless, in some cases, you will need to provide additional information such as your mobile number and location. You can also follow up with Kenya Power through the accounts to know how far your case has reached. Among the three options, social media is the most effective.

    Call Customer Support

    You can reach customer support via 95551 any time of the day throughout the week. Also, you can call 0703070707 or 0732170170. Remember, to use this service you will need sufficient airtime.
