    Many of the most famous handbags in the world have been named after a woman with iconic style. This includes the Birkin, the Kelly, the Jackie – and of course the Lady Dior, which was named (or more accurately, renamed) after Diana, the Princess of Wales. The handbag is synonymous with the royal’s wardrobe and remains one of the house’s most well-known styles, a coveted treasure that will truly never go out of style.

    The history behind the Lady Dior is fascinating, with the house renaming the style after Diana because she loved it so much. According to the brand, it was in September 1995, while attending the opening of the Paul Cézanne retrospective at the Grand Palais in Paris, that the First Lady of France presented Diana with a brand new Dior bag, which was then called Chouchou – a style that had not yet been released.

    Tim Graham//Getty Images

    Just a few months later, the princess was pictured carrying the bag as she stepped off a plane in Buenos Aires – but this would not be the only time she was seen with the Chouchou. In fact, she became somewhat attached to the style, carrying it with her time and time again. When Diana decided that she would also love to have the bag in navy, to match her eye colour, the house officially renamed the bag the Lady Dior, a playful nod to the handbag's most famous fan, Lady Diana Spencer.

    When Diana attended the Met Gala in 1996, fashion history was made. The royal carried the mini Lady Dior, a smaller version of her favourite handbag, which had been created especially for her in blue satin to match her dress. It became one of her most significant fashion moments, and the pictures are constantly revisited in celebration of her famous style.

    Courtesy of Dior

    There is little surprise that Diana became so attached to the bag – there was nothing quite like. A super ladylike style, the Lady Dior can be recognised by its boxy shape, rounded top handles, Cannage motif stitching across it and its delicate lucky charms, which are a nod to Christian Dior, who carried charms around with him everywhere he went.

    Over the years, the legendary bag has blossomed, released in a multitude of colours, sizes and materials, being reinvented season after season. Now available in micro, mini, small, medium and large models, as well as the horizontal D-Joy format in everything from patent leather to printed tulle, there are countless iterations of the bag, including some true collectors' items from the Lady Dior Art Project (an annual event, which sees some of the world's most exciting artists enlisted to reinvent the handbag through their own artistic vision.)

    © ANDREA CENETIEMPO

    Almost 30 years after it first launched, the Lady Dior now has countless A-list fans, from Anne Hathaway, Kelly Rutherford, Blake Lively, Dakota Johnson and Sarah Jessica Parker to Rihanna and Priyanka Chopra – and it has become a favourite not just for day-to-day street style, but for the evening too. That is the beauty of the Lady Dior, as proven by its namesake, who owned various sizes and carried them everywhere from work engagements to the Met Gala.
