    Early Life

    Billy Gardell was born on August 20, 1969, in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, making him a proud native of the Steel City.

    Stand-up Beginnings

    Gardell discovered his passion for comedy at a young age and began performing stand-up comedy in local clubs around Pittsburgh.

    Breakthrough Role

    Gardell gained widespread recognition for his role as Officer Mike Biggs on the hit CBS sitcom “Mike & Molly,” which aired from 2010 to 2016.

    Comedy Central Presents

    In 2006, Gardell appeared on Comedy Central’s “Comedy Central Presents” where he showcased his comedic talent to a national audience.

    The Billy Gardell Show

    Gardell’s own sitcom, “The Billy Gardell Show,” aired briefly in 2006 and allowed him to further showcase his acting skills.

    Host Extraordinaire

    Gardell has served as the host of numerous shows, including the revival of the game show “Monopoly Millionaires’ Club.”

    Big Screen Debut

    Gardell made his feature film debut in the 2002 sports comedy “Bad Santa” alongside Billy Bob Thornton.

    Voice Acting

    Gardell lent his voice to the character of Santa in the animated holiday movie “Ice Age: A Mammoth Christmas.”

    Comedy Album

    In 2014, Gardell released a comedy album titled “Billy Gardell: Halftime” which showcases his hilarious and relatable stand-up comedy routines.

    Touring Comedian

    When he’s not busy with his acting career, Gardell continues to tour the country as a stand-up comedian, bringing laughter to audiences everywhere.

    Family Life

    Gardell is happily married to his wife, Patty Knight, and they have a son named Will Gardell.

    Pittsburgh Sports Fan

    Being a Pittsburgh native, Gardell is an avid supporter of the Pittsburgh Steelers and Pittsburgh Penguins.

    Comedy Influences

    Gardell draws inspiration from legendary comedians such as George Carlin, Don Rickles, and Jackie Gleason.

    Love for Music

    The Billy Gardell Podcast

    Guest Appearances
