    Idina Menzel | Broadway, Movies, Music and Business

    Broadway

    It is no secret that the Frozen actress initially started out in the live-screen theater.

    Her first major role came in 1995 with “Rent,” which was her first professional theater job and not to mention her Broadway debut.

    For this performance, she was nominated for the Tony award.

    However, for her next major Broadway breakthrough, Idina waited for another eight years.

    In 2003, she starred in the Broadway musical “Wicked” which garnered widespread critical acclaim.

    This role also earned Idina her first Tony Award for Best Leading Actress in a Musical.

    Movies

    As mentioned multiple times before, Idina is best known for voicing the Disney princess Elsa in the Frozen franchise.

    However, she started out in minor supporting roles on the small screen in the early 2000s.

    Her first major role was in the silver screen adaptation of her first Broadway play ‘Rent’ which left many critics lauding.

    Similarly, in 2007 Idina appeared in her first Disney musical film “Enchanted.”

    After that, she went on to serve in several recurring roles in different TV series, most notably “Glee.”

    And since her iconic voice in Frozen, her roles on the big as well as the small screen have revolved around the Frozen franchise.

    Music

    Everybody knows Frozen’s mesmerizing soundtrack, ‘Let it Go,’ which went on to win a Grammy and an Academy Award.

    But throughout her career, Menzel has released six studio albums to date.

    Following her Broadway debut with Rent, she released her first solo record, “Still, I Can’t Be Still,” in 1998.

    She then followed it with “Here” in 2004, which also contributed to the soundtrack of Desperate Housewives.

    Idina’s third solo album titled “I Stand” was released in 2008 and debuted at number #58 on Billboard.

    Her fourth album titled “Barefoot at the Symphony” was also the first of her two live albums.

    She then released a self-titled studio album in 2016, which debuted at number #29 on the Billboards.
