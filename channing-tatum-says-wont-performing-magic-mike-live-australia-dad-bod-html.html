    Channing Tatum has revealed why he won't be performing in Magic Mike Live when it hits Australian shores next year: he's got a 'dad bod'.

    During an appearance on Sunrise with Magic Mike's choreographer, Alison Faulk, the 39-year-old confessed he no longer has the same physique he had in the movies. 

    '[I've got a] dad bod, man! I got a kid now, this is what it is,' he said, referring to his six-year-old daughter, Everley, whom he shares with ex-wife Jenna Dewan. 

    That's life! While promoting Magic Mike Live on Sunrise on Wednesday, Channing Tatum (second from left) revealed he's got a 'dad bod' because he no longer works out

    'I can't have a full-time job of being in the gym anymore!' he added.

    When asked what it takes to be a Magic Mike Live performer, Channing said: 'It is a full-time job. It isn't just working out, it is diet, it's everything. It is a lifestyle.

    'What you see in Magic Mike [the film] is me holding it together for two weeks. As soon as they cut in the last scene, I'm like, "Where are the cheeseburgers?"' 

    Letting himself go: 'I can't have a full-time job of being in the gym anymore!' said Channing. Pictured in 2012 film Magic Mike

    While he only got in shape for a movie role, Channing says the performers in Magic Mike Live are 'like Olympic athletes' all year round. 

    The Australian version of the widely-popular stage show will include 162 dates next year in Melbourne and Sydney, with further dates for Brisbane and Perth to follow.

    While Channing won't appear in the shows, Magic Mike Live features 20 performers, including 15 'of the most talented male dancers in the world'.

    Dedication: While he only got in shape for a movie role, Channing says the performers in Magic Mike Live are 'like Olympic athletes' all year round 

    The performances will take place in the purpose-built, 600-seat Arcadia venue, which has been described as the 'world's largest Spiegeltent'.

    The show will tour six months in Melbourne, before travelling to Sydney for shows from November 19 at the Entertainment Quarter in Moore Park. 

    Tickets, which range between $75 and $185, are available at Ticketmaster.com.au
