    It's been over 25 years since the cast of The Thin Blue Line made their television debut on BBC1 in November 1995.

    Spanning across two seasons, the popular British sitcom - set in a police station in the quaint town of Gasforth - brought fans comedy heavyweights such as Rowan Atkinson and some newcomers onto the scene. It was also given spot 34 in a list of Britain's Best Sitcoms.

    Throughout the show, there were plenty of laughs and awkward moments as Inspector Raymond Fowler's team locked horns with Detective Inspector Derek Grim.

    While the show would come to an end just before Christmas in 1996, some of the stars of the show were destined to go onto bigger and better-acting endeavours within the last two and a half decades.

    Here, we take a look at where the cast is now.

    Inspector Raymond Fowler - Rowan Atkinson

    Old-school officer Inspector Fowler was a key character in the entertaining happenings that took place across the two seasons.

    Played by comedy star Rowan Atkinson, the actor had already firmly established his name in the entertainment world after appearing in Blackadder and Mr. Bean.

    While his portrayal of Inspector Fowler was not set to be his most memorable character, the actor has continued to enjoy comedy work across television and stage ever since.

    For his noteworthy film appearances, Atkinson has starred in Four Weddings and a Funeral, Scooby-Doo, Love Actually and the Johnny English film series.

    In 2013, he was awarded an OBE for services to drama and charity.

    Atkinson - who has been dating actress Louise Ford since 2014 - also became a dad for the third time in 2017 and the couple is said to split their time between London and Oxfordshire.

    In 2021, the actor also added that he had looked forward to ending his iconic Mr Bean character, telling Variety: "I don’t much enjoy playing him. The weight of responsibility is not pleasant. I find it stressful and exhausting, and I look forward to the end of it.”

    Sergeant Patricia Dawkins - Serena Evans

    Played by actress Serena Evans, Sergeant Dawkins was known as Inspector Fowler's girlfriend of 10 years and a character notoriously terrible at cooking.

    While Serena had starred in some minor roles ahead of her BBC debut, it would be The Thin Blue Line that she was most well known for.

    She has also made brief appearances in Casualty, Lewis, and Catastrophe.

    As well as her television appearances she also frequently starred in stage productions such as The Merry Wives of Windsor that was put on at Shakespeare's Globe in 2010.

    Detective Inspector Derek Grim - David Haig

    Boofoonish character Detective Inspector Grim - played by actor David Haig - is desperate for promotion and often locks horns with Fowler.

    Haig is not only an actor but also a playwright and has enjoyed almost four decades of work in the entertainment world.

    At the time that Derek had been cast in the show, he had already received an Olivier Award and enjoyed several successful television and film appearances, such as Four Weddings and a Funeral alongside Rowan Atkinson.

    It appears that Derek has contributed to a number of roles alongside Rowan since the sitcom, including a hilarious 2007 Comic Relief sketch called Mr. Bean's Wedding in which he appeared as the bride's father.

    Throughout the last few years, the actor has also landed roles in several coveted spots including a re-make of Yes Prime Minister, Killing Eve as well as the Downton Abbey movie.

    It was also in 1996, when Haig and his wife Julia Gray were rocked by the devastating tragedy of losing their daughter Grace. The tiny tot was tragically stillborn on Derek's birthday in 1996. The couple has since become patrons to the organisation SANDs in order to support families also going through the same loss.

    PC Goody - James Dreyfus

    Actor James Dreyfus had already enjoyed success on stage before he was cast in Absolutely Fabulous and then The Thin Blue Line and Gimme Gimme Gimme.

    Known for starring as P.C Goody in the hit sitcom, the character is flamboyant and verges on childish as he tries to meander his way through work. Often getting on the nerves of Inspector Fowler, he certainly knows how to press his boss' buttons.

    Following his role in Thin Blue Line, James was cast as Martin in Notting Hill and has also starred in television shows such as Midsomer Murders, Casualty, Shameless and kids' show The Sarah Jane Adventures.

    In 2020, he came under fire for being one of 50 actors and writers that penned their support for JK Rowling after she faced backlash for her views on the trans community.

    PC Habib -Mina Anwar

    Mina Anwar played P.C Maggie Habib in the two seasons of The Blue Thin Line. Considered one of the more brainy officers among Fowler's unique team, the character was a popular addition to the cast.

    The Thin Blue Line was one of Anwar's first breakout roles and she has since gone on to enjoy roles in The Bill, Doctors and Nurses and several roles in kids' TV.

    She has also had brief appearances on Coronation Street, Shameless, and Doctor Who. For her latest acting endeavor, Mina was announced to be starring in the cast of The Importance of Being Earnest, which will take place in Leeds.

    Aside from her acting work, Mina was also known to have launched Aware International in 1999 which was a range of underwear that had secret pockets to store condoms in.

    PC Gladstone - Rudolph Walker

    81-year-old actor Rudolph Walker is known for his work in comedies such as The Thin Blue Line, Love Thy Neighbour and Ali G Indahouse.

    The iconic star assumed the role of veteran office PC Gladstone across the two seasons, known for coming up with some bizarre suggestions and impersonations.

    Following his success in the BBC sitcom, Walker went on to enjoy several television roles before being cast as Patrick Trueman in EastEnders - a role which he has had since 2001.

    A few years ago, the actor was awarded the Outstanding Achievement Award at the British Soap Awards before receiving a CBE in 2020.

    Aside from his ongoing acting work, Rudolph has also set up The Rudolph Walker Foundation which aims to support disadvantaged youths get into the entertainment world.
