    West African baby names are an important ritual in the life of Africans with far-reaching implications. In a society where the meaning of a person’s name is said to have a bearing on the character or fate of the individual, it becomes necessary to give children names that can build their lives. In addition to that, many rules govern how children are named. Some common criteria include the gender of the baby, time of the year the baby was born, and season. Other people consider notable occurrences during that time like the death of a relative or leader and the future role of the child in society.

    A name provides the primary way of identifying a person in Africa. It not only show heritage but is also essential in identifying the family tree. If you are keen enough, a name will probably give you the time frame which a person was born and the place. This is because African names are usually given according to seasons, and it is common to have people of the same age group sharing names. It is obvious therefore that a name links a person to a family, tribe or society. African boy names meanings usually depict strength and power as opposed to those of girls that tend to focus on beauty and agility. Below are West African baby names and their meanings.

    1. Abayomi

    This is a unisex name that has its origins in the Yoruba people of Nigeria. It means ‘born to bring me joy’. It is one of the most common baby names, given that children bring joy to their families. Many families feel the need to name their children so that they are reminded of their significance.

    2. Abimbola

    Abimbola is a girl name among the Yoruba of West Africa, Nigeria. It means ‘born to be wealthy’. Who wouldn’t want their children to be wealthy? The name is given to children whose future riches can be seen from the present such as those born of royalty.

    3. Ade

    Ade is a Yoruba name for male children. The name means ‘royal’. It is a name that was given to children born into the royal families or those who were thought to be noble enough to fit the title. Children given this name were tasked with leadership roles in future lives. You can give your newborn child this awesome name if you think he will be a great leader in the days to come.     4. Adebayor

    The Yoruba name for male children means ‘he came in a joyful time’. The significance is more on the timing and what it means to people around. The child is born at a time when everything is as it should be and everybody was anticipating for it. Those children given this name tend to bring joy wherever they go at a time when it is most needed.

    5. Akanni

    Akanni is a male baby name common in the Yoruba tribe of Nigeria. It means ‘our encounter brings possession’. It attributes to one having the luck of bringing riches to those they encounter.

    6. Akanke

    The female baby moniker Akanke has its origins among the Yoruba of Nigeria. It means ‘to know her is to love her’. It is a title that reminds us not to judge people before we get to know them. We can only love people after we have known their character and personality.

    7. Adamma

    Adamma is a female baby moniker that means child of beauty. This title is from the Igbo people of Nigeria. It is a common moniker for girls who have inherited their beauty from their parents. Just like the saying an apple doesn’t fall far from the tree, it is believed that people called Adamma will have beautiful children as well.

    8. Akumsinachi

    The Ibo moniker Akumsinachi for baby girls means ‘my wealth is from God’. It is a title that reminds people to be thankful to God for all the things they have. It is a good moniker, especially for very religious people. If you are amazed by long African names, Akumsinachi is one to consider when naming your child.

    9. Azzam

    This is an Arabic moniker common in Ghana. It is a title for male children meaning ‘one who is determined and resolved’. People given this moniker tend to have a strong personality and do not give up easily when faced with challenges. Azzam can easily replace African names that mean warrior due to the determining factor.

    10. Addae

    This nice moniker for boys has its origins among the Akan people of Ghana. It means ‘morning sun’. Sunrise signifies many good things in the African context. The morning sun gives light that is essential for life. It is also associated with beauty, authority and resilience.

    11. Azhar

    Azhar is an Arabic moniker that is common in Ghana. The title means ‘shining’ or ‘luminous’. It is given to male children who impact positively on others. They are people who bring joy to others even when they require the affection of others.

    12. Babafemi

    ‘My father loves me’, is the meaning of the moniker Babafemi. The title is found in West Africa among the Yoruba of Nigeria. It is common to find it given to children who do not have siblings because if there are more than one children, others may feel not loved.

    13. Bobo

    Bobo is a male moniker that means ‘born on Tuesday’. This title originated from the Fante people of Ghana. It is for those parents who have the habit of marking important days in their lives. In this respect, they would like to commemorate the day their child was born, and if that day is a Tuesday, then the baby boy is called Bobo.

    14. Baderinwa

    The female moniker Baderinwa means ‘worthy of respect’ among the Yoruba of Nigeria. It is a female title that alludes to the inalienable right of dignity accorded all people by birth. As a result, people ought to be respected regardless of their economic or social background.

    15. Bejide

    Bejide is a Yoruba name for females that means ‘born during a rainy season’. The title is a reminder of life like a double-edged sword. The rainy season can be good as it provides water for agriculture and other domestic uses. The same rain can also be catastrophic by bringing floods and diseases. Whichever way, the meaning of the moniker has some significance for which it has to be remembered.

    16. Chimeremeze

    The Ibo moniker Chimeremeze is given to boys. It means ‘my God made me king’. It is also a reminder of a people’s belief in God as the supreme being. Through God’s will, he can give people power and respect. Even though African American baby boy names may lack a heritage connection, monikers such as Chimeremeze should be considered for their meaning and significance.

    17. Chibuzo

    Chibuzo is a moniker both for boys and girls. It is found among the Igbo people of Western Africa. The title means ‘God leads the way’. People called Chibuzo tend to put God first in anything they do and are spiritual warriors in the eyes of others.

    18. Chidiebere

    Among the Igbo people, Chi means God. Chidiebere is a title shared by both boys and girls. Its meaning is ‘God is merciful’. Sometimes the name acts as a reminder that children are gifts which they do not deserve but have by God’s mercy. If you are intrigued with African last names, this is a good choice for your child.

    19. Dubaku

    Dubaku is a unisex title among the Akan of West Africa. Its meaning is ‘the eleventh born child’. It is not uncommon to find one family with more than ten children in Africa. When God has blessed you with the eleventh child, then Dubaku is one of the baby monikers to consider for your boy or girl.

    20. Ejiroghene

    This Urhobo moniker comes with a certain mint of sophistication, but it has religious significance. Its meaning is ‘praise God’. The moniker can be shared by boys and girls. It is often given to children to remind people of the difficult process of labour, and thus, God deserves praise when a child is born without any problems.

    21. Enitan

    This title is found in West Africa, and it means ‘person with a story or storied person’ in the Yoruba language. It is a unisex title and is usually given to children whose birth is extraordinary or has some significance to the family and community. It means the story of the child will be told for generations. People called Enitan tends to be famous.

    The Igbo title Funanya is given to females and means ‘love’. Parents of children called Funanya celebrate the special bond between them and the child’s uniting factor. It is a reminder that the child is a fruit of love and one who brings happiness in the home.

    23. Ifiok

    Ifiok is a West African title found among the Ibibio people. It is a title not reserved for any gender, therefore, can be given to either a boy or girl child. It means ‘wisdom’ and is given to children whose parents think are going to be wise in future. People called Ifiok are usually consulted when making important decisions in the family.

    24. Ime

    Ime is an Ibibio word that means ‘patience’. It is given both to boys and girls. It means the parents of the child have waited a long time to conceive the child. People known as Ime are also known to be meticulous in what they do as they like to be organized.

    25. Jatau

    Jatau is a Hausa title for males that means ‘fair-coloured or light’. It attributes to the skin colour and is given to those whose skin tone is lighter. It is one of the many African names for boys that give preference on the appearance rather than one’s character.
