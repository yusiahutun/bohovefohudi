    Amita Suman says 'someone like her' is not supposed to be living their dream

    01 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Amita Suman has given an insight into her life before her rise to stardom as she appears on the cover of Tatler.

    The star of Netflix series Shadow and Bone, 25, revealed that she was born in Bhedihari, southern Nepal, a village which had just 800 houses and a population of less than 6,000 people.

    She added that her birthplace, where she lived until she was seven, had no running water, electricity or school so she did not have an education in her early life.

    Posing in a chic white jacket as well as a jewel-encrusted flapper-style dress, Amita said: 'Someone like me isn't meant to be living their dream. I really, really am. And I'm not complaining.'

    She added: 'Maybe because I've had such a crazy life, it's forced me to mature a lot quicker than the average person. I hate saying that. 

    Humble beginnings: Amita Suman has spoken about her rise to stardom after growing up in a small village in Nepal with no running water as she appears on the cover of Tatler

    'But I've been through a lot in life. I remember so much of it, because it was so different. We had absolutely nothing.'

    Her childhood also saw her have a brush with death, as she came close to being bitten by a highly venomous cobra. 

    She told the publication that she froze as she was not afraid of dying but of being bitten- but her grandfather saved her life after battering it with a stick.

    Amita said they told her: 'Don't move! Amita, do not move an inch!' as they fought back against the animal, who had killed two villagers that week.

    After her humble beginnings, the actress, who also posed in Tatler wearing a playful patterned green and blue dress and pink boots, first pursued an interest in being on the small screen when she saw a television for the first time.

    A villager brought one out during a festival and she said: 'I really can't remember what it was, or what I saw: all I remember is how it made me feel. I thought there was another planet inside this TV. 

    'I started touching it and talking to the people. I asked, 'Can you hear me? Show me how I can be there with you. I used to sit with my grandfather for hours and pray to be that person on that TV. It was then I knew what I wanted to do in my life.'

    When Amita was seven, her family moved to Brighton and she had to learn English, revealing that she was introverted and insecure when she arrived because she could not speak the language.  

    Chic: The Shadow and Bone star, 25, revealed that she was born in Bhedihari, southern Nepal, a village which had just 800 houses and a population of less than 6,000 people

    The star, who trained at the Academy of Live and Recorded Arts, said: 'I felt like such an outsider in this country but I found my self-worth in drama.

    'It gave me this freedom and confidence to live life through something else and forget about the person I was. 

    She revealed that her stepmother told her: 'if that's what you want to do, that's what you will do.'  

    Amita graduated in 2018 and landed minor roles in Casualty and Ackley Bridge.

    After a few other roles, it was announced that Amita was going to be featuring in Shadow and Bone.

    The adaptation of the fantasy book series The Grisha Trilogy and the Six of Crows Duology by Leigh Bardugo was a huge hit with fans.

    She said: 'I will never do it again, but I put my name in the search bar and the response was a big breath of relief.

    'Everyone was relatively happy. It was a big bang of a start into what this world [of fantasy] was going to be like and how much it meant to all of these people. 

    Stunner: Posing in a jewel-encrusted flapper-style dress, Amita said: 'Someone like me isn't meant to be living their dream. I really, really am. And I'm not complaining'

    Rise to fame: After a few other roles, it was announced that Amita was going to be featuring in Shadow and Bone, which was a huge hit with fans (pictured on the show in 2021)

    'So I felt accepted, but I will never give that power to anyone else. 

    'The pressure was insane; I was nervous on set every single day, but it was the best experience of my life.'

    The star also admitted that she likes to head out clubbing sometimes and often goes to the iconic Clapham haunt Infernos.

    She said: 'I mean, it can be fun. We go in there to be really silly and look absolutely bonkers on the dancefloor and not give two sh**s about anybody else. It's amazing.'

    See the full feature in the April issue of Tatler available via digital download and on newsstands from Thursday 2nd March
