    The city was founded on March 15, 1822.

    Ponta-grossês is a distinct regional dialect that incorporates elements of Portuguese, German, and Italian due to the diverse heritage of the local population.

    Yes, Ponta Grossa is known for its vibrant cultural festivals, such as the Festa Nacional do Milho (National Corn Festival) and the Festival de Dança (Dance Festival).

    Ponta Grossa has a rich historical background and was a important location during the Paraguayan War. The city was also a key center for the industrialization of Paraná state.

    The local cuisine in Ponta Grossa is a mix of traditional Brazilian dishes with a regional twist. Popular dishes include the “barreado” (beef stew) and “pudim de leite” (caramel custard).
