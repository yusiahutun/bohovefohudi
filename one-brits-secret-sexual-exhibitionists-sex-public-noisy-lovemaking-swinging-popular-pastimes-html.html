    Whether screaming down the house while having sex, doing it in public places or swapping partners at parties, exhibitionism in the bedroom department is apparently more popular around the UK that one may think.

    One in four Brits would class themselves as secret sexual exhibitionists, according to a new study.

    And when it comes to sexual confidence in the bedroom, the average woman would rate hers as a 7.5 out of 10, while men gave themselves just 6.1.

    More than half of the people who claimed to be 'secret sexual exhibitionists' enjoyed having sex in public places

    The study, conducted by ukmedix.com, polled 2,010 adults across Britain, and 26 per cent claimed they would class themselves as a sexual exhibitionist. Of these, 79 per cent said their exhibitionism was a secret from others.

    When asked what form their exhibitionism took, the most popular answer amongst respondents was 'having sexual relations in public', with 56 per cent of the 'exhibitionist' respondents claiming to do so.

    One in three claimed to be 'purposefully loud' during lovemaking, and one in ten claimed to partake in 'group love' sessions.

    One in three claimed to be 'purposefully loud' during lovemaking, and one in ten claimed to partake in 'group love' sessions

    When asked if they had ever been 'caught in the act', 62 per cent of the self-confessed exhibitionists said yes - and of these, 13 per cent claimed that they had been caught by a family member.

    When asked why they enjoyed secret exhibitionism, two-thirds admitted it was the 'thrill' it gave them; a fifth (19 per cent), liked the idea of being caught.

    There were three in the bed...

    However, 34 per cent of the exhibitionists were alone in this, admitting that their partner didn't personally enjoy exhibitionism.

    Thomas O'Connell of ukmedix.com commented: 'The secret sex lives of people around the UK are always a bit of a mystery, so we wanted to look at the trait of exhibitionism, along with how confident people feel when getting it on.

    'To see that as many as one in four of us are exhibitionists, but are likely to keep it secret, was surprising - clearly a lot of us like the idea of being 'caught in the act' and the thrill it might give!

    'As long as you're not putting yourself in any bad situations, there's nothing wrong with a bit of exhibitionism.

    'Just ensure your partner feels the same and you're not doing anything illegal, as the thrill you get from being 'caught' may not outweigh the repercussions!'
