    Dolores Catania revealed that she aims to continue to lose more weight, despite already dropping '20 pounds' using both Ozempic and Mounjaro drugs.

    The Real Housewives Of New Jersey star, 52, who sparked backlash when she admitted to taking Ozempic in April, gave an update on her weight loss journey while talking to The US Sun in an article published on Sunday.

    The TV personality opened up to the outlet at a glitzy party for her new BELLA Around Town magazine cover earlier last week.

    In regard to her progress, she said, 'I’m down 20 pounds,' but added that although she currently weighs 137, her goal is still 'to be in the low 130s.'

    Catania told the publication that she has been using the drug called Mounjaro, which is a type 2 diabetes treatment, similar to Ozempic, which she quit taking.

    Weight loss journey: Dolores Catania, 52, revealed that she aims to continue to lose more weight, despite already dropping '20 pounds' using Ozempic and Mounjaro drugs; seen in June in NYC

    The reality star added, 'I'm being realistic. Right now, I'm 137. I was 157 when I started,' and she further explained that in the past, she 'jumped to 163 — I was going there. Like, I was gaining weight every month.'

    Dolores stated that losing weight was challenging, expressing that, 'It doesn't happen overnight. It doesn't come off easy.' 

    Before making the switch to Mounjaro, Catania revealed, 'I took Ozempic, stopped and then begun Mounjaro. I was medically prescribed for both because I am insulin resistance/pre-diabetic and have a thyroid issue.'

    The mother of two admitted that, 'You know, everyone's losing so much weight [on it],' but added, 'It's questionable — what are the repercussions of this when everyone's done? Is there side effects? All the doctors that I spoke to said "No."'

    Other celebrities have publicly admitted to using Ozempic, including Chelsea Handler, Sharon Osbourne and also Golnesa 'GG' Gharachedaghi of Shahs Of Sunset fame. However, other Real Housewives stars, including Kyle Richards, have adamantly denied taking the weight loss drug. 

    The TV personality then explained what prompted her to stop taking Ozempic after two years and revealed, 'it was expensive and everything.'

    Despite using Mounjaro, Dolores told The US Sun that she still follows a strict diet and fitness routine in her daily life to achieve her slimmed down figure.  

    'But being healthy and living a healthy lifestyle is cheap — it's free! I work out regularly. There is no easy fix for weight loss and I have to watch what I eat.'

    Back in April, Dolores appeared on Watch What Happens Live with Andy Cohen, where she admitted she had been taking Ozempic to slim down for the three-part Real Housewives Of New Jersey reunion, which first aired in late May.

    She told the host, 'I wasn’t going to come to the reunion looking any bigger than anyone else, so I got on the bandwagon.' 

    Catania then stated that she had actually switched to Mounjaro at the time and added, 'The reunion is in two weeks. I do have a trainer [as well].' 

    The star's statement to Cohen soon sparked immediate backlash from fans, with some jumping to Twitter to share their opinions on the matter. 

    One tweeted, 'Dolores is proudly using ozempic, those side effects will kick in later on, laugh now cry later #WWHL,' while another penned, 'Dolores saying it's #Ozempic so proudly just took me out lol smh #WWHL.' 

    A social media user also wrote, 'Again, I love Dolores, but ew that she's gloating about being on ozempic for weight loss #RHONJ.' One typed, 'Dolores just admitted to being on Ozempic.' 

    Soon after, Catania jumped to her own defense while appearing on Page Six's Virtual Reali-Tea podcast, and explained that she was taking the weight loss drug for health reasons. 

    'I’m going through menopause, I have a thyroid issue, I have inflammation and weight is hard to take off,' she stated. 'And I work out, and I don’t eat crazy, and I was gaining weight, and it’s a lot of different layers...Let’s just let everybody know.'

    According to The US Sun, Dolores has also gotten cosmetic procedures done over the years, such as Botox and liposuction back in 2020 after losing 25 pounds at the time. 

    Along with focusing on her fitness journey, the reality star revealed her special summer plans to the outlet. Catania will soon whisk herself away to Ireland for a memorable trip, which is also her boyfriend Paul Connell's home country. 

    She happily gave an update on their relationship, and stated, 'Things are going really good. He's been able to handle all this [fame] a lot better than most would. He has fun with it.' 

    The happy couple were first romantically linked in 2021, and went Instagram official last year in February 2022. Dolores was previously married to Frank Catania II from 1994 to 1998, and they share two children: Gabrielle, 27, and Frankie, 24.
