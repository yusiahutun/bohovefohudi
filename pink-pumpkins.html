    Pink Pumpkins are Real and They're Beautiful

    18 Mar 2024, 00:00

    We've seen so many stunning ways to decorate porches this fall, from monogram mum displays to galvanized gourds, but this new breed of pumpkin may be the prettiest yet.

    Farmer Troy Starner and student Dawson Garvick are growing beautiful pink pumpkins on Troy's farm in New Oxford, Pennsylvania, to raise funds for breast cancer foundations, WGAL News reports. They're among a group of 50 farmers across America growing the unique gourds for the Pink Pumpkin Patch foundation this fall.

    [facebook ]https://www.facebook.com/PinkPumpkinPatch/photos/a... data width="800"[/facebook]

    This year alone, the Pennsylvania duo produced 400 "porcelain doll" pumpkins on a half-acre patch for the charitable cause. The men were inspired to get involved because Troy's mom and stepmom, and Dawson's mom, have all been afflicted with breast cancer.

    [facebook ]https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=1020201065... data width="800"[/facebook]

    [facebook ]https://www.facebook.com/PinkPumpkinPatch/photos/a... data width="800"[/facebook]

    Pink Pumpkin Patch founder Carol Froese, a breast cancer survivor herself, cultivates the colorful pumpkin seeds. "Our pink pumpkins provide a fun and meaningful opportunity for people across the country to support breast cancer research," Carol said. The annual campaign launched in 2012 and it encourages people to not only buy the pumpkins, but also to display them on their porch to show unity in the fight to find a cure.

    You can find these pretty pumpkins at Walmart, Kroger, Cub Foods, the Home Depot, and other grocery stores across the country, or learn more through the Pink Pumpkin Patch foundation.
