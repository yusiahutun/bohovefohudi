    List of Most Expensive Private Universities in Kenya 2022

    Universities in Kenya have been pushing for an increase in school fees to offset the increasing cost of operation due to the high cost of living. Private Universities recently resorted to increasing school fees for government-sponsored students joining in September 2022.

    26 Apr 2024, 00:00
    • The Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service (KUCCPS) announced the placement of the 2021 KCSE students by September 2022.
    • Most universities and colleges are prepared to receive the first lot of students, both private and government-sponsored students
    • Private Universities raise school fees above the government-recommended KSh 16,000 tuition fee per semester
    • The Catholic University of East Africa (CUEA) and Daystar University are the latest to have increased tuition fees by around KSh 20,000

    Universities in Kenya have been pushing for an increase in school fees to offset the increasing cost of operation due to the high cost of living.

    Private Universities recently resorted to increasing school fees for government-sponsored students joining in September 2022.

    Fee increment

    This came despite the Ministry of Education directive on fee increment, preventing any learning institution from raising fees without any consultation.

    The Commission for University Education accredited over 30 private universities to operate in Kenya.

    Gov't sponsorship

    The universities were incorporated into the placement system by KUCCPs in 2016 and have so far enrolled 47,548 public-sponsored students.

    The government set the tuition fee for undergraduate degree students at KSh 16,000 across the board.

    But private institutions have been raising this figure by an average of KSh 20,000.

    So, what are the most expensive private universities in 2022?

    1. Daystar University

    Tuition fees per semester for undergraduates student at Daystar University is calculated based on credit hours, usually 12.

    For instance, in Nairobi, each credit hour is KSh 6,410, according to ugfacts.net. This translates to KSh 76,920 per semester.

    2. Kabarak University

    The tuition fee at Kabarak University ranges from KSh 60,000 to KSh 80,000 per semester.

    This range is determined by the degree programme you are undertaking.

    For example, the Bachelor of Education Arts tuition fee is KSh 65,000 while the Bachelor of Science in Computer Science is KSh 70,000.

    3. Catholic University of East Africa (CUEA)

    The tuition fee for the bachelor's degree programme at CUEA is KSh 6,000 per unit and KSh 8,000 for graduate school.

    If you take 12 units per semester, your tuition fee comes to around KSh 48,000.

    4. Mount Kenya University (MKU)

    The university tuition fee is charged depending on your degree programme.

    For instance, a Bachelor's degree in Arts cost KSh 45,000 per semester, while a degree in Nursing will cost around KSh 99,000, according to ugfacts.net.

    5. Strathmore University

    The tuition fee at Strathmore University varies depending on the degree programme.

    For example, a low student pays around KSh 200,000 in tuition fees per semester.

    Hiked school fees

    In related news, TUKO.co.ke reported private universities are raising school fees for government-sponsored students joining in September 2022.

    Law students joining the Catholic University of East Africa (CUEA) will pay KSh 46,000 up from KSh 24,500.

    Daystar University increased school fees by around KSh 17,000, above the government-recommended semester fee of KSh 16,000.
