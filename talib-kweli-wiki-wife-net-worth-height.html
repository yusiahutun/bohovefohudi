    How did Talib Kweli start his Professional Career?

    In 1996, Talib made his debut on the rap scene. He was a featured artist on the Doom album by the Ohio and Cincinnati band Mood. In Cincinnati, Talib and DJ Hi-Tek first connected. These two collaborated on projects like Reflection Eternal later in their careers.

    A return to New York for Talib occurred between 1998 and 2000. The band Black Star was founded by him and Mos Def. Hi-Tek, who also produced the album, gave it the name Mos Def and Talib Are the Black Star. In 1998, this album was released.

    On 2000, Talib and Hi-Tek issued an album titled Train of Thought. In the year 2000, Mos Def and Talib worked together on the Hip Hop for Respect initiative. This was mainly done to draw attention to the Amadou Diallo case. Talib and Mos contributed songs to the 2001 CD Red Hot + Indigo.

    In 2002, Kweli issued his debut album by himself. He contributed to Kanye West’s critically acclaimed debut album, The College Dropout. Talib has worked with Rawkus Records, Blacksmith Records, and his own record label, Javotti Media, which he established in 2011. Talib Kweli is renowned for his activism and charitable endeavors.
