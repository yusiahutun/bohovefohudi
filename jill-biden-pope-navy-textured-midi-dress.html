    Adam Lippes on How It Feels to Dress the First Lady

    New York designer Adam Lippes was in a hotel room in Washington, DC early this morning, drinking coffee and scrolling through the news on his phone, when he saw his own navy suit on First Lady Dr. Jill Biden as she shook hands with Pope Frances at the Vatican.

    14 Sep 2024, 00:00

    New York designer Adam Lippes was in a hotel room in Washington, DC early this morning, drinking coffee and scrolling through the news on his phone, when he saw his own navy suit on First Lady Dr. Jill Biden as she shook hands with Pope Frances at the Vatican.

    “I’m not an emotional guy, and I was really emotional this morning,” he said to Bazaar.com in a phone call. “In fashion we put so much love into what we do, and to have the first lady wear us when she could wear any great designer — there’s so much great stuff out there — it’s really moving.”

    Franco Origlia//Getty Images

    The First Lady and President Joe Biden met with the Pope to kick off their trip to Europe, which is only the second foreign tour of Biden’s presidency so far. The moment had particular historic significance; it’s been over half a century since the last time Catholic U.S. President sat down with the leader of the Catholic church at the Vatican.

    For the occasion Dr. Biden wore a textured navy sheath dress by Lippes with a matching blazer over it, along with a pair of navy Marion Park heels. Over her hair, she pinned a black lace mantilla by Gigi Burris, conforming to Vatican protocol that requires women to cover their heads when meeting with the Pope.

    Vatican Pool

    The dress is typical of the First Lady’s taste, which runs towards the sophisticated but classic — a description that could apply just as well to Lippes, who specializes in understated luxury. He learned the trade at Ralph Lauren, spent years at Oscar de la Renta, and launched his own line in 2013. His pieces are elegant, with an emphasis on quality over extravagance. They’re the sort of thing you might pick to meet the Queen for tea, as the First Lady did back in June, the first time she wore Lippes’s brand.

    Pool/Max Mumby

    Dr. Biden wore the navy suit throughout the day Friday, going from the Vatican to a café with Brigitte Macron, the wife of French president Emmanuel Macron, for a glass of wine.

    “The first lady has surprised me with how she puts things together herself,” says Lippes. “What I respect so much is how much she’s been rewearing things. Which is how people live! They’re not styled head-to-toe four times a day, and they don’t need to be. I really think she’s been making her own choices, as she should — she’s Dr Biden, after all.”

    Alessandra Tarantino

    Dr. Jill Biden meets with Brigitte Macron in Rome.

    Indeed, the First Lady has said that she doesn't work with a stylist. She’s also been clear that she knows her support can be game-changing for a brand, adding “I like to choose from a diverse group of designers.” She has a history of repping smaller designers and brands, including New York-based labels Brandon Maxwell and Markarian, which she chose for the inauguration.

    “That is SO important,” says Lippes. “Having the First Lady wear American brands that maybe people are not exposed to as much is incredible for our industry. Bravo to that.”
