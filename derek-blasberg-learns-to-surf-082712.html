    I had one goal this summer: Learn to surf.
    Well, I'm happy to report back that I did just that. (To be fair: I'm no
    Kelly Slater, but I did manage to stand up on the board and ride a few
    waves.) I can't take all the credit for it, however.
    Corey Senese, a Southern California boy who grew up on the waves with a
    hardcore surfer father, had a lot to do with my novice attempts. After a
    week of lessons out in Montauk, I sat down with the teacher (whose website has more information) about my performance and everything else he's seen from his perch in front of the waves.

    Derek Blasberg: Corey, tell me how I did! On a scale of 1 to Kate Bosworth in Blue
    Crush    , what kind of surfer am I?

    Corey Senese: You were really good! You had
    some of the easiest surf there is to learn on, so you lucked out. I
    have to tell you that you would
    have gotten smashed if the waves were chest high. So I'd put you in the
    70 percent level.

    DB: I'll take a C average, since I didn't study! Is August the busiest time of year for you?

    CS: Yes, by far. It's when it's the warmest, but it can get very crowded.

    DB: Where do you go when its cold?

    CS: For a couple of months to San Diego, which is near where I grew up and first learned to surf.

    DB: Where are the best waves on Long Island?

    CS: Where the swell hits the coast at the perfect angle, which is different for every swell.

    DB: Where are the best waves in the world?

    CS: Indonesia. Definitely.

    DB: What's the best surf or biggest wave you've done?

    CS: Perfect Blacks Beach in San Diego. The waves were fifteen feet high and perfect.

    DB: When did you start to surf, and when did you decide it was something you wanted to do for a living?

    CS: I moved on to surf boards after boogie
    boarding. I was probably 5. I never thought about it as a career, so I
    just sort of fell into it.
    I'm very lucky.

    DB: I brought my 5-year old friend Stella on a lesson with me. Is it easier to start surfing as a kid?

    CS: This is the best way for a kid to get
    into the sport: Learn how to swim, then learn how to boogie board, then
    surf! Kids usually
    do take to it quicker than an adult once they are confident in
    negotiating waves.


    DB: What's the first thing that anyone who wants to surf should do? (For me, it was look for cute wet suits, but you thankfully had one for me.)

    CS: Do some research and take a lesson or two with a really good surf instructor.

    DB: And finally, teach me some surf lingo. Do people still say, 'surf's up'?

    CS: Totally! Like when you got pulled into
    the tube and got hit in the head by the lip and then got sucked over the
    falls and ate it on a
    rock. Surf's up!

    DB: Ha! I didn't say 'surf's up' when that
    happened. What I said was a little more colorful. What about 'hang ten'?
    Do people still say that?

    CS: Hang ten toes over the nose!
