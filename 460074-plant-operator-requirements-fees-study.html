    The demand for plant operators is on the rise in Kenya. Plant operators are professionals who monitor and maintain equipment at power and chemical plants, oil refineries, and other factories. Before becoming one, you need to pursue a plant operator course in a reputable institution.

    TABLE OF CONTENTS

    Plant operators use heavy machinery to dig, lift, and move materials in factories and plants. You can also find them working on building and agricultural sites. Before you pursue a plant operator course, you should have an idea of the machinery you wish to specialise in. Some specialise in excavators, others giant cranes, and others choose other types of machines.

    Plant operator course: all you should know

    You must attend a college or training institution to gain the right qualifications before you can be a recognised plant operator. Below are all the details you should know about the plant operator course, requirements, fees, and institutions that offer this programme.

    Requirements for a plant operator course

    A plant operator oversees the day-to-day operations of earthmoving machines and lifting equipment. As a plant operator, you will learn how to operate different types of machinery and equipment.

    Some equipment are used in construction, while others are used in factories and agriculture. If you want to become a plant operator, you must complete training with a registered institution. You will receive a certificate after completing the programme.

    It is important to note that the requirements vary from one college to the other. Below are the general requirements to pursue this course.

    • A valid national ID card
    • A valid driver's license (Applicants without a license are required to undertake a one-month driving programme)

    NB: Academic certificates (KCPE and/or KCSE) may or may not be a requirement.

    Course duration

    The plant operator course is classified as a short course. It takes between one and three months to complete this programme.

    The duration depends on the institution you choose. Soon after completing the course, you will be required to go for attachment.

    Plant operator course fees

    The fees for this course vary depending on the institution of your choice. The amount may also vary depending on the types of machines you wish to specialise in.

    Some institutions require accommodation fees (for boarders), while others do not offer accommodation. Generally speaking, the training fee ranges between KES 60,000 and 120,000.

    Plant operator schools in Kenya

    There are multiple institutions offering the plant operator course. Some are new, while others have been operational for a long time and have a great reputation. Below is a look at the top institutions you can enrol in for this course.

    1. KIHBT plant operator course and requirements

    KIHBT is an acronym for Kenya Institute of Highways and Building Technology. It is a public institution that provides high-quality training in infrastructure-related courses.

    The institution offers the plant operator course on the Ngong Campus. This course is offered every two months, meaning it takes two months to complete it.

    KIHBT equips trainees with the relevant knowledge and skills to operate various road construction equipment and machinery.

    The KIHBT plant operator course requirements are a valid ID and a valid driver's license. The skills and knowledge offered include the areas listed below.

    • Choice of plants and uses
    • General maintenance of machinery and simple repairs
    • Highway code and safety
    • Machine operation and control
    • Good workmanship and precision

    2. Sensei Institute of Technology plant operator course

    Sensei Institute of Technology is a modern college that offers training in technical skills. The institution offers courses in driving, catering, ICT, mechanics, electrical and electronics, plant operator, and building and construction courses.

    The institution offers the plant operator course on the Nairobi and Nakuru campuses. The campuses are well-equipped with forklifts, motor graders, cranes, motor rollers, dumpers, excavators, farm tractors, paver machines, track shovels, and backhoe loaders, among other machines.

    How long does a plant operator course take?

    The course takes between one and three months to complete in Kenya, depending on your preferred institution.

    What are the qualifications for a plant operator?

    The primary requirements are a valid national ID/ passport and a driver's license. Some institutions may have additional requirements.

    Which schools offer plant operator course in Kenya?

    The institutions that offer this course include International Plant Machinery Training Technical Institute, Kilimambogo Highways Building and Technology, Sensei Institute of Technology, and Kenya Institute of Highways and Building Technology.

    How much is a plant operator paid in Kenya?

    Plant operators in Kenya earn between KES 17,836 and 80,309 per month. Generally, new employees are paid less than their seasoned counterparts.

    Is plant operation marketable in Kenya?

    Yes, the course is marketable in the country. Numerous factories, construction zones, and agriculture sites need qualified plant operators.
