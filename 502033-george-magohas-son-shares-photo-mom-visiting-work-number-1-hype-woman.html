    • Michael Magoha, the son of late former Education CS George Magoha, has shared a photo of his mother for the first time since burying his dad
    • The Nigerian-Kenyan widow visited her son at his workplace, and he was elated and praised her, saying she was her number-one supporter
    • Magoha passed away on January 24 following a heart attack and was buried on February 11 in Siaya county

    It has been 61 days since former Education Cabinet secretary George Magoha was buried at his ancestral home in Umiru, Siaya county.

    Magoha passed away on January 24 after suffering a cardiac arrest. He left behind his widow Barbara Odudu, and a son, Dr Michael Magoha.

    The Nigerian woman elicited mixed reactions among Kenyans with her cute dance styles during her man's funeral, and she defended why she did not mourn him as expected in some cultures.

    Barbara Magoha, the loving mum

    On Wednesday, April 12, the handsome doctor was elated after his mum visited him at his workplace.

    Barbara, who is also a doctor and known for her love for bright colours and popping lipsticks, wore a colourful skirt suit and red lipstick.

    "Number one hype woman came to visit at work today," Michael Magoha captioned the photo.

    Magoha and Barbara met at Lagos University in 1972 and tied the knot in 1982.

    Barbara eulogises Magoha

    In February, before Magoha was buried, Barbara paid a glowing tribute to him.

    She took a walk down memory lane, remembering the great moments she shared with Magoha since the first day they met at Lagos University in Nigeria.

    "Exactly one month after we met, he started telling everyone we were going to get married, and that was the first time we had a quarrel. I asked him 'how can you do that?' and he told me, 'I know you would not accept because I'm not Nigerian. So go and tell them why you can't marry me'".

    The mother of one showered her husband with love, saying she chose to settle down with him because of his family values.

    "He was a faithful man who put his family first. "He was an extremely jovial man, and he used to tell me I must go before you because if you go before me, I would not last. That was always his prayer," she said.

    The two were married for 40 years.
