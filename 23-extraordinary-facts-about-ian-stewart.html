    FAQs

    Q: What is Ian Stewart best known for?

    A: Ian Stewart, also known as “Stu,” is best known as the original keyboard player for The Rolling Stones. He played a significant role in shaping the band’s sound and was an essential member during their formative years.

    Q: Was Ian Stewart a full-time member of The Rolling Stones?

    A: Although Ian Stewart contributed greatly to The Rolling Stones’ music and was regarded by the band as a key member, he was not officially recognized as a full-time member. He served as a touring and session musician, providing unmatched piano skills that added depth to their music.

    Q: What other instruments did Ian Stewart play?

    A: Aside from his exceptional piano skills, Ian Stewart was also proficient in playing organ, harmonica, and percussion. His versatility as a musician allowed him to contribute to various musical styles and genres beyond his work with The Rolling Stones.

    Q: Did Ian Stewart have any solo projects or albums?

    A: While Ian Stewart primarily focused on his work with The Rolling Stones, he did release a solo album titled “Boogie with Stu” in 1985. The album showcased his virtuosity on the piano and featured collaborations with notable artists.

    Q: What philanthropic endeavors was Ian Stewart involved in?

    A: Ian Stewart was heavily involved in charitable work, particularly in the field of healthcare. He actively supported the British Heart Foundation before his unfortunate passing in 1985, leaving behind a legacy of both musical greatness and philanthropy.
